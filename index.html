Trench Grade

Survey the run with your phone, set the fall, read the dig depth at every point — house to ditch.

1Survey the trench line

How it works: plant a stake with bold markings (or clip a tape measure to it) at the start of the run, markings measured up from the ground. Walk the trench line holding the phone at the same height every time — resting it on top of a pole is best, eye height works too. At each point, hold the phone upright like taking a photo, tilt until the line turns green (level), and read where the line crosses the stake. Enter distance + reading below.

— °

Before you start: hold the phone flat against a door frame or window jamb (anything you trust to be plumb) and tap Calibrate — that zeroes the tilt sensor for sighting. Sanity check: standing right next to the stake, the green line should read exactly your phone height.

Lay the phone flat on a straight board (a 4-ft level or 2×4 is ideal), top of the phone pointing toward the ditch. Start at the house. Set the board down on the ground, tap Capture, move the board one length forward, repeat until you reach the ditch.

Calibration: put the board on a surface you trust to be flat and tap Zero on flat once before you start. To check the zero, capture a spot, spin the board 180°, capture again — the two angles should be equal and opposite.
